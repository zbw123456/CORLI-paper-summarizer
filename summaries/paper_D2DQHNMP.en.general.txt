HistBERT is a new version of a language model called BERT, which is designed to work better with older texts. Most language models like BERT are trained on modern writing, but HistBERT is trained on historical English from the Corpus of Historical American English. This corpus includes texts from different time periods, so HistBERT can understand how word meanings change over time.

The researchers wanted to see if training on historical data would help with tasks like finding similar words or tracking how word meanings shift over time. They compared HistBERT to the original BERT and found that HistBERT performed better in these tasks. This suggests that using a model trained on historical texts can give more accurate results when studying how words change in meaning over long periods.

The study also highlights that the time period of the text you use matters. If you're studying old texts, a model trained on modern language might not work as well. HistBERT shows that adapting the training data to match the time period of your study can improve results. This is useful for researchers in digital humanities or historical linguistics who need to analyze how words have changed over centuries.
