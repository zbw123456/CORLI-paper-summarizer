This study explores how non-native English speakers (NNESs) use AI tools to rewrite sentences while maintaining their original meaning. The researchers created ParaScope, an AI assistant that offers different ways to check and understand AI-generated paraphrases, such as translating sentences back into the user’s native language, providing explanations, or showing example sentences.

In their experiment with 22 NNES participants, the team found that people’s preferences for these tools depended on their English skill level. Users often started by looking at general information before diving into more detailed explanations. Back-translation—seeing their sentence translated into their native language—was the most commonly used feature, but it wasn’t the only factor in deciding whether to accept a suggestion. Instead, users combined multiple tools to make their choice.

The study suggests that AI paraphrasing tools can help NNESs feel more confident, work more independently, and write more efficiently. However, the researchers also warn that too much information can overwhelm users. They recommend designing these tools carefully to avoid confusion while still providing useful support.

Based on their findings, the authors offer practical advice for creating better AI writing assistants that help NNESs make informed decisions without feeling lost in too much data.
