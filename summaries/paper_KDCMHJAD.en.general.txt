This study looked at how Japanese university students learning English as a foreign language participated in vocabulary tasks using smartphones and games. The researchers used Complexity Theory, which examines how small changes in connected parts of a system can lead to big differences in outcomes. In this case, the system included the students, their personal traits, the classroom setting, and how they interacted with each other.

The study involved an action research project where students created their own vocabulary quizzes on their phones. The goal was to see how these factors influenced how much students participated and what they learned. The findings showed that students’ language growth wasn’t steady or predictable. Instead, it varied a lot depending on their starting points and the support they received from the teacher. The study also found that there were many ways for students to succeed in the task, suggesting that viewing tasks as dynamic systems could be helpful for teachers.

The research was part of a larger conference volume, but this specific study highlights how technology, collaboration, and individual differences all play a role in language learning. It suggests that teachers should be flexible and adapt their approaches based on the unique needs of their students.
