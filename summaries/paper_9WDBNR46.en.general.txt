This paper explores how artificial intelligence (AI) and natural language processing (NLP) tools are changing the way researchers study language using large collections of text, called corpora. The study reviews 12 research papers published between 2013 and 2025 that use models like BERT and GPT to analyze language in new ways. These AI models help automate tasks such as finding key words or identifying speech acts, making it easier to study large amounts of text quickly and with deeper meaning.

The research finds that AI tools can improve traditional methods in corpus linguistics by adding speed and accuracy. For example, they can track how language changes over time or compare how people speak in different social groups. Tools like CorpusChat and Hugging Face Transformers are often used to support these analyses. However, the study also points out important challenges. AI models can sometimes reflect biases from their training data, and their decision-making processes are not always clear, which can make it hard for researchers to trust or explain their results.

The authors conclude that while AI offers powerful new ways to study language, researchers must be careful about ethical concerns, data privacy, and making sure their findings can be reproduced. They suggest that future work should focus on creating AI models that are more tailored to specific types of language research and easier to understand. This would help ensure that AI tools are used responsibly in academic studies of language.
