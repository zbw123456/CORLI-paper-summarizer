We study metadata conditioning as a lightweight approach for localizing large language models (LLMs). The research design involves pre-training 31 models from scratch at two parameter scales (0.5B and 1B) on large-scale English news data annotated with verified URLs, country tags, and continent tags. The dataset covers 4 continents and 17 countries, enabling controlled experiments to assess the impact of metadata conditioning on geographic localization.

For model choices, we train decoder-only transformer models with metadata conditioning mechanisms integrated into the input embeddings. The conditioning is applied by concatenating metadata tokens (e.g., country or continent tags) to the text tokens during pre-training. We compare models trained with metadata conditioning against baseline models trained without metadata, as well as region-specific models trained exclusively on data from a single region.

The evaluation strategy includes four controlled experiments. First, we measure in-region performance by evaluating models on held-out data from the same regions they were trained on. Second, we assess cross-region generalization by testing models on data from unseen regions. Third, we compare the performance of global models with metadata conditioning to region-specific models to determine if conditioning can recover localization comparable to dedicated regional models. Fourth, we evaluate learning efficiency by analyzing the rate of performance improvement during training with and without metadata conditioning.

Ablation studies are conducted to isolate the contributions of different metadata sources. Specifically, we compare models conditioned on URL-level metadata alone, country tags alone, and continent tags alone to determine the relative importance of each signal. Additionally, we examine the impact of balanced regional data coverage by training models on subsets of regions and evaluating their ability to generalize to held-out regions.

For the downstream benchmark, we introduce a dataset of 800 localized news multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering diverse geographic regions. After instruction tuning, we evaluate metadata-conditioned global models on this benchmark and compare their performance to LLaMA-3.2-1B-Instruct. The benchmark assesses the models' ability to answer region-specific questions accurately, providing a practical measure of localization effectiveness.

Assumptions include that the metadata tags (URLs, country, continent) are accurate and representative of the geographic distribution of the news data. We also assume that the decoder-only transformer architecture is sufficient for capturing the benefits of metadata conditioning without requiring architectural modifications. The evaluation assumes that the MCQ benchmark is a valid proxy for real-world localization performance.
