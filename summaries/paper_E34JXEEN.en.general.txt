This paper explores how artificial intelligence can help create English language learning exercises quickly and effectively. The researchers used advanced language models to generate sentences tailored for learners at different skill levels. Their goal was to ensure the sentences were grammatically correct, varied in difficulty, and suitable for testing specific grammar rules.

The team developed methods to control the output of the language models, making sure the generated sentences matched the needs of language learners. For example, they could adjust the complexity of sentences for beginners versus advanced students. They also focused on creating sentences with different grammatical structures to help learners practice specific concepts.

To test their approach, the researchers asked human evaluators to rate the quality of the generated sentences. The results showed that the sentences were grammatically sound, with scores of 3.4 or higher out of 4. The sentences for advanced learners were also longer (24% longer) and more complex (9% more complex) than a basic version of the system.

Overall, the study suggests that AI can produce high-quality, controlled language learning materials efficiently. This could be useful for teachers, app developers, and anyone creating educational content for English learners. The researchers assume that the language models they used were already trained on large amounts of English text, which is typical for such systems.
