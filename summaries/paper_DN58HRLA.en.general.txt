Data accuracy is a big challenge when working with large datasets. Errors in data can lead to poor research results, unreliable applications, and flawed machine learning models. Manual checking is often impossible because the datasets are too large. This paper introduces a method to automatically improve data accuracy over time through repeated testing and correction.

The approach is iterative, meaning it keeps refining the data step by step. The key idea is to use tests that identify errors efficiently, reducing the number of mistakes faster than random checks would. The paper proves mathematically that this method will eventually remove all errors from the dataset, assuming the tests are reliable. Simulations and a real-world example support these claims.

In practice, this means organizations can trust their data more over time without needing constant human oversight. The method works best when the tests used to find errors are accurate and cover the most common mistakes. The paper doesn’t specify exact test types, so the exact setup would depend on the data being used.
