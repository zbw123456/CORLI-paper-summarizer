The research design employs a likelihood ratio-based approach to measure translationese on a graded scale rather than a binary classification. The core methodology involves fine-tuning two language models (LMs) contrastively to distinguish between original and translated texts. The translationese-index (T-index) is derived from the likelihood ratios produced by these models, providing a continuous measure of translationese presence.

For model choices, the authors utilize two 0.5B parameter LMs fine-tuned on synthetic data pairs ranging from 1k to 5k instances. The contrastive fine-tuning involves training the LMs to maximize the likelihood of original texts while minimizing it for translated texts, or vice versa, depending on the direction of the likelihood ratio. This setup ensures that the models learn nuanced linguistic features associated with translationese.

The evaluation strategy includes two primary components: generalizability testing and validity assessment. Generalizability is tested across unseen genres, authors, and language pairs using both synthesized translations and real-world translations. The validity of T-index is validated against human judgments, including pointwise ratings and pairwise comparisons, to ensure alignment with human perception of translationese.

Baselines for comparison include traditional MT quality estimation (QE) metrics such as BLEU and COMET. The study finds low correlation between T-index and these baselines, indicating that T-index captures aspects of translationese not covered by existing QE metrics. This suggests that T-index can serve as a complementary metric in MT QE, providing additional insights into the quality and characteristics of translated texts.

Assumptions include the effectiveness of synthetic data for fine-tuning the LMs and the representativeness of the evaluated domains and language pairs for generalizability. The study does not explicitly detail the specific synthetic data generation process or the criteria for selecting language pairs and genres for evaluation.
